Example: Business Direction for Petroleum Industry
Example of a grounded vision for a petroleum company.
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Business Direction
Main Description

Vision Statement
First in production with the best people

Values

  • Our primary measure of success is how well we meet production targets relative to cost.
  • Our primary asset is innovative and results oriented people who are prepared and encouraged to make good decisions and execute timely actions.
  • We recognize our people based upon their personal accomplishments and how they contribute to business results.
  • We have significant pride and take responsibility for what we do and how we do it.
  • Leadership in developing and applying technology and know-how is the foundation for our success.
  • We are sensitive to the well-being of our people and our communities.

Mission Statement
To produce the required volumes of petroleum and gas in the most efficient, profitable and secure manner.

Goals (excluding indicators and objectives)
Client Stakeholder

  • Deliver products on time, on spec
  • Achieve high satisfaction levels
    • Customers
    • Suppliers
  • Improve working relationships with the unions and community

Financial

  • Reduce cost in real terms
  • Meet budget requirements

Operations

  • Generate required production capacity
  • Produce required barrels of crude oil, LPG and gas
  • Increase operational efficiency
  • Reduce accidents

Innovation/Learning

  • Improve effectiveness and accessibility of technology
  • Improve quality and diversity of skills

Critical Success Factors (we must)

  • Demonstrate flawless performance in meeting commitments
  • Be viewed as fair and equitable by our suppliers, the unions and communities we serve
  • Manage and control variable costs
  • Optimize recovery rates of reservoirs
  • Minimize unplanned downtime of facilities
  • Improve safety awareness and maintenance
  • Capitalize on leading edge production and decision support technologies
  • Improve education and training content and delivery

Strategies

  • Establish service level agreements and client/stakeholder council (top 50) by ____
  • Implement supplier certification program/incentives, jointly with suppliers (top 10) by ____
  • Establish “workforce” of the future to engage union in support of the vision (top 2) by ____
  • Drill X new wells in priority a fields 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 by ____
  • Expand secondary recovery to priority b fields 2, 7, 9, 14, by ____
  • Implement process management: Production units by ____, Service units by ____
  • Outsource planning and delivery of non-core competency education and training by ____
  • Install new EIS, Scada and Telemetry and GIS technology in priority fields by ____
  • Assess the accessibility of our public and employee systems by ____

Management principles

  • Work together towards our vision and goals
  • Ask daily “What have I accomplished and contributed to the business?”
  • Solve problems when and where they occur
  • Don’t wait for someone else to do what you could or should do
  • Take ownership and be proud of what you do
  • Focus on putting oil in the tank
  • Treat ABC’s money like your own
  • Always strive to improve yourself and the way you work
  • Safety and quality is everyone’s job
  • Treat others as you wish to be treated
  • Focus on people’s abilities, remove accessibility barriers
