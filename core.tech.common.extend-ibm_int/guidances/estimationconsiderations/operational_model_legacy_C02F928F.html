Main Description

Creating Operational Models is very time consuming depending on the size and complexity of the system. The minimal time required to construct an Operational Model is normally 1 month. It is not unusual for the Operational Models to take 1 year on a large engagement.

Significant factors that influence the OM development effort include:

  • Technical complexity,
  • Number of nodes and deployment units. A more complex solution will usually have a larger number, requiring a greater effort to define and document.
  • Degree of distribution of execution and data DUs across the OM. A complex solution may require additional architectural effort.
  • Demanding system management requirements. Issues such as high levels of availability, performance or security make the definition of this work product more complex.

Assumptions:

  • A1: Work products, on which this work product has a dependency, have been produced to the appropriate standards. If these are not fully covered, or at less than full depth, expect to require more effort in this work product.
  • A2: There is no existing systems management strategy to work from
