Guideline: Architectural Evaluation of Components
This guideline offers guidance on an architectural evaluation of candidate components.

Main Description

In the course of making architectural decisions, it is often necessary to compare two candidate components or to evaluate a component with regard to non-functional requirements that have been established for the solution. This guidance supports such a comparison or evaluation by providing a comprehensive set of architectural aspects of a component and guidance on evaluation of each aspect.

Within this guidance, the term component generally refers to any software product, component or other executable asset. While the term component typically is used in the context of application or infrastructure software, this guidance may be at least partially helpful when evaluating certain hardware components such as appliances.

This guidance is intended to support the IT architect in evaluating a candidate component. When completed, the evaluation should result in a compilation of concise architectural information about the component. This information can provide standard input necessary to evaluate, compare and select components for inclusion in a solution design. It can be particularly useful in making architectural decisions and development of the component and operational models. For components under consideration, the evaluation can be used to:

  • Understand the primary capabilities, structure and how the component is typically applied.
  • Determine suitability for non-functional and operational requirements including such factors as capacity, performance, standards, target platforms, constraints and component interfaces.
  • Identify similar components from IBM and other vendors and establish a basis for comparison.
  • Identify component issues that affect risk

Although this guidance provides a comprehensive basis for evaluation, any aspect of the evaluation may indicate an inability of the component to meet important requirements. Consequently it may be helpful to prioritize the aspects of evaluation to consider the most important requirements first. If all of the most important requirements are met, subsequent aspects of the evaluation may be used in a comparison of alternatives.

The information in this guideline is closely associated with the IBM Architecture Brief. The Architecture Brief is published for selected IBM software products and may be found at the  SWG Blueprint
