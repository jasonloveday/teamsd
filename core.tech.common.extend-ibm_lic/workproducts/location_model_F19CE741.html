Artifact: Location Model (ART 0523)
This artifact provides a model of the locations across which the business and IT operate, a description of their characteristics and capabilities, and a description of the relationships between locations.
Purpose

The purpose of this artifact is to provide a framework for the analysis, placement, deployment, and subsequent management of a system’s or enterprise’s elements such as business resources, systems, applications, data, networks, & other technology.

This artifact may be used as an analysis tool in its own right. For example, it may be used to optimize the locations across which the business and IT operate. Or this artifact may be used as a backdrop to show visual representations and analyze the placement of business and IT resources.  For example, the following may be mapped to this artifact;

  • Business components, business activities, business roles, business processes, business functions, business users, business transaction volumes, financial metrics, actors, IT system infrastructure, networks, zones, and IT system transaction volumes.

This artifact may be developed and used in many different contexts.  For example;

1. In the context of the design and development of an IT system:

  • As a way of setting geographic and physical business constraints and business operational requirements for the system.
  • To assist with the development of non-functional requirements.
  • As a key input to defining the physical distribution of the nodes, deployment units, and connections that together form the operational aspect of the architecture of the IT system.
  • As a key input to developing and cross-cutting views of the architecture and design of the IT system (e.g. performance, availability, systems management, and security cross-cutting views).
  • To provide an input to creating the technical deployment, roll-out, and training plans.

2. In the context of a network architecture and design project: to model existing communications:

  • To specify future communication requirements, and as a backdrop for the architecture and design of the network.

3. In the context of business strategy, business architecture, and business transformation projects:

  • To facilitate the analysis of business resources and to drive the definition of placement guidelines that will be used by IT system development teams.
  • To analyse the behaviour of the business from a geographical dimension with a view to optimizing the business operations.

4. In the context of IT strategy, IT architecture, and IT transformation projects:

  • To analyse and optimize the IT operations with a view to rationalising and consolidating systems / applications / and the IT property portfolio.

5. In the context of IT system service provision:

  • To enable inventory management, asset management, and facilities management, property management, auditing, and compliance.

Description
Main Description

This artifact provides a model of locations and describes the locations’ characteristics and capabilities, and the relationships between locations. 

A location is a geographic place, such as: a user’s desk, a room, an office, a floor of a building, a building, a city, a country.

This artifact represents all locations relevant to the project context including customer, supplier, business partner, agent, and other service provider locations.

In the context of the operational aspect of the architecture of an IT system, the relationship between two locations defines the communication support between the locations and is expressed as a border.

During the development of this artifact it may be useful to develop different defined levels of this artifact:

  • Logical – a classification (or a category) of geographic places where the business operates, based on the types of business activities that are performed at them.
  • Physical - a real place where the business has a presence.
Brief Outline

Depending on the context this artifact (or its contained artifacts) may include:

  • A summary of the context in which this artifact is being developed and used and what it will consist of, and to what level of detail.
  • A relationship diagram (or diagrams) showing the relative geographic positioning of locations, the relationships between locations, and showing the communication support between locations.
  • A description of the locations and their characteristics and capabilities.
  • A description of the borders between locations showing the communication support between locations.
Notation

This artifact is defined using text, matrices and diagrams, i.e., in a "rich" picture. 

In many cases, this artifact will exhibit complex inter-relationships and communications which if drawn as a “box and line” diagram will exhibit crossing lines.  A specialized notation should be used for the relationship diagrams facilitating a clear view of the support for communication between locations (expressed by “borders”), and to facilitate the overlay of other architecture elements of interest such as users, deployment units, nodes, and connections.

The notation is defined by the IBM Architecture Description Standard Notation for Operational Model V2 and the IBM System Description Standard V3.
Key Considerations

This artifact may be developed to different levels of detail and contain different content depending on the context in which it is being developed or used.  For example, in the context of a business strategy project, this artifact may not contain a description of the borders.

Locations may be nested - one location may contain another location. For example, a city may be defined as a location and then contain an office location, or a secure cabinet may be defined as a location within a machine room.

A single office building may be defined as multiple locations – one for each floor of the building.

This artifact models and describes all locations that are relevant to the context including, for example customer, supplier, partner, agent locations.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without this artifact it is hard to understand and analyse the proper geographic placement, deployment, and subsequent management of business resources and IT resources such as IT platforms, applications, and data.

There will not be a clear understanding of the geographic placement of business and IT resources.

In the context of IT systems development:
  • It will be difficult to understand the physical requirements and constraints that need to be taken into account when developing an IT system, nor model and analyze the behavior of the IT components from a geographic perspective in order to achieve the IT system’s functional and non-functional requirements within the constraints of technology, skills and budget.
  • It will be difficult to develop a view of the site and facilities management requirements and components, to plan site surveys and roll-outs, and to effectively plan training schedules for users. 
In the context of business strategy, architecture and transformation:
  • It will be difficult to develop a clear understanding of where the business operates from and to optimize the business locations.
In the context of IT strategy, architecture, and transformation:
  • It will be difficult to develop a clear understanding of the placement and geographical distribution of IT systems, applications, application functions, technical infrastructure, data, and actors.
In the context of networking strategy, architecture, and design:
  • It will be difficult to set a network strategy, develop a network architecture, nor design and specify a network.
In the context of business operations, IT operations, and service delivery (also referred to as service provision):
  • It will be difficult to maintain configuration, asset and inventory management databases, plan end-user computing deployment initiatives, provide support to users, nor to assess impact of network and system failures on business users.
Reasons for not needingThis artifact may not be needed for business and IT organizations which operate from a single location.
Representation Options

This artifact may be represented in any combination of the following; a drawing tool (e.g. Visio), a word processing tool (e.g. MS Word), and a spreadsheet tool (e.g MS Excel), and a database tool.
