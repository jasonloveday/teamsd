Task: Describe Current IT Environment and Plans
This task will describe current the client's current IT environment including standards, currently installed technology and architecture.
Disciplines: Environment
Purpose

The purpose of this task is to:

  • Reach understanding of the technical environment that the solution will be supported by.
  • Be a reference point for the project team for technical issues as the team introduces new solution components.
  • Determine any constraints in designing and deploying the application produced by this environment.
  • Document standards as they are formally identified in the client's current architecture information and as they are actually practiced by developers and lines of business.
  • Identify any current IT plans which might impact potential opportunities.

      Main Description

      In the process of understanding the official (documented) IT environment and standards, we should also understand the currently installed technology and de facto standards.  If an Enterprise Architecture exists, standards, principles, data placement guidance, etc. should be captured during this task.
      This task is performed by:

      • Surveying and documenting the client's inventory of information systems technology and standards.
      • Creating an inventory of the installed base of hardware, applications and other technical capabilities in the enterprise as both a text-based report and a graphical depiction of the environment.  
      • Reviewing reports from previous workshops such as the Component Infrastructure Roadmap (CIR) or Assessment & Roadmap Tool / Technical Health Assessment (ART/THA) for information about the clients current and target capabilities and any IT transformation roadmaps recommended to them.

      The output artifact of this task may be summarized and analyzed through use of the Assessment & Roadmap Tool / Technical Health Assessment (ART/THA) of IT infrastructure or of IT competency. The ART/THA can then be used to help Task: Identify Opportunity.  The ART/THA assessment produces (among other things) a heat map positioning the clients IT capabilities where IBM has strengths, weaknesses, opportunities or threats. That heat map is determined by the gaps in capabilities and IBM's occupancy in each capability vs. the competition.

      As was the case with other tasks that help us understand the client's business and organization, we will limit our scope to the aspects of the IT environment that are likely to be important as we identify opportunities. 

      Key Considerations

      Like other tasks in the Plan Phase of Team Solution Design, this is an on-going process.  If there is a teamroom or community used to store client information, the results of this task will be stored there for use on later projects.

      "Additional effort on behalf of a specific opportunity will typically be limited to what is required to identify opportunities and no more.
