Team Solution Design

Main Description

December 2016:

Following are the updates made as part of V2.3 Release:

  • Updated Activities to adopt Agile Method Framework's approach where each activity has a primary purpose to accomplish and this is manifested in a table of Objectives, Guidance Materials and Tips/Considerations.
  • Addition of guidance:
    • Discovery MindMap that contains the type of information to be gathered or understood to better understand client needs.
    • Developing a Customer Point of View to provide a compelling business-oriented recommendation for customers regarding a specific business problem. The PoV is expected to be a discussion starter and a door opener to pursue further discovery and validation activities with customers.
    • Conducting a Customer Workshop in collaboration with clients to address the impact of business and technology shifts to their business and showing how we can help.
    • Solution Design Recipes which are scenario-specific prescriptive guidance with outcomes, elements or “ingredients” and steps on what to do.
    • Effective use of IBM Design Thinking in support of Solution Design via an updated guideline and new companion whitepaper.
  • Updates to Guidelines and Whitepapers which will be made available in the Integrated Cognitive Solution Design community in the Method Supplemental Guidance Wiki. This reorganization allows for more frequent updates to ensure currency of the guidelines and whitepapers.

October 2015:

This second iteration of the TeamSD Next Method V2.2 includes :

  • Updates to reference additional techniques and capabilities to use in Technical Account Planning and Ideation, which were the new Tasks added with the launch of TeamSD Next.
  • The new techniques and resources referenced are: Enterprise Value Points, Innovation Thinking, Business Innovation Architecture and Use of the IBM Client Centers.
  • A new Guideline for Business Innovation Architecture.
  • Reference to current practice for customer surveys in the Activity: Confirm Client Value and Experience.
  • Updates to reference Cloud considerations in the Activity: Develop High Level Operational Model
  • Text and image updates to a few tasks and activities for currency and consistency with TeamSD Next course material.

The Modernization of TeamSD is also accompanied by the new Guidance module for TeamSD that is provided within Solution Seeker. Both V2 of TeamSD and the new Guidance interface are referred to as TeamSD Next. 

June 2015: 

There are 2 New Tasks added in TeamSD V2.1:

  1. Ideation (within the Explore Activity): This new task explicitly adds to TeamSD the need for IBM technical leaders to bring fresh ideas to clients in a collaborative setting, rather than expecting a Client Initiative to be well defined.  It features a subset of IBM Design Thinking techniques as a leading way to approach this task.
  2. Plan for Client Value (within the Understand Activity): This replaces the optional sub-activity Develop Transformation Roadmap previously in TeamSD
    The new task features more specific guidance on the type of planning that provides a context for specific client initiative and related sales opportunity.
    The emphasis is on Technical Account Planning and the new Work Product: Technical Account Plan.

TeamSD continues to evolve to incorporate Guidance and Task Description updates for CAMSS:

  • An update to Define Key Services (a Task within the Develop Activity) to reflect the emergence of SaaS
  • A Guidance paper on SaaS and TeamSD produced by members of the technical community is included
  • The Big Data/Analytics/Big Data paper has a major update.
  • Several other Task Descriptions are updated with references to Cloud or Analytics consideration.

August 2010:
The following changes were included in version 1.0.4 of Team Solution Design.


Several artifacts and their associated tasks and guidance were updated to support the SA4TeamSD configuration of IBM's Rational System Architect (hereafter referred to as SA4TeamSD). The affected artifacts include the following.

  • Business Direction

  • Component Model

  • Non-Functional Requirements

  • Operational Model

  • Service Model

  • Subject Area Model

  • System Context

  • Use-Case Model

  • Viability Assessment


The changes to these artifacts and their associated tasks and guidance are summarized as follows.

  • Minor changes were made to terminology to ensure consistency with SA4TeamSD and IBM's System Description Standard (SDS) R3.

  • Term Definitions were added for the concepts of Access Mechanism (new) and Exchange (revised).

  • Additional guidance was added to explain system context modeling (supported by SA4TeamSD) which is an optional, more formal approach to defining system context.

  • Where appropriate, illustrations were replaced by equivalent illustrations rendered using SA4TeamSD.

  • Where appropriate, examples were replaced with similar examples developed using SA4TeamSD.

  • For some complex examples, the original example was retained and a second example from SA4TeamSD was added.


These changes do not affect the overall TeamSD delivery process and do not require the use of SA4TeamSD for solution design.
