Task: Identify Opportunity
This task will help clarify the opportunity for those situations where the opportunity is complex, not well defined or likely to be a compound opportunity with multiple significant aspects.
Purpose

Once you have taken the overall view of where IBM's capabilities can deliver Client Value, you will begin to work on specific opportunities. The need to take special measures to identify the opportunity will vary considerably. The purpose of this task is to deal with those situations where the opportunity is complex or not well defined. This task will provide approaches to clarifying opportunities or dealing with complex or multiple opportunities. If the opportunity is well defined and agreed to with the client, this task may be optional.

Main Description

It is critical that you consult the Technical Account Plan for context for an Opportunity, insight on the client's technical environment and the sequence of initiatives, or roadmap, towards overall Client Value. When you are providing a Solution Design for a client proposal, there is a broader context to consider and the Technical Account Plan is a primary reference for this.

Identifying the opportunity can range from a simple to a very complex task. The complexity will vary in part based on the client's buying behavior in a specific situation. Buying behaviors are defined as a part of the Client Value Method (CVM). They are summarized here.

  • Value for Money:  Client wants a specific technology or service from a trusted brand configured and delivered to their specifications on time at competitive prices, simply and efficiently.
  • Trusted Supplier:  Client wants innovative, best in class technology and support services, integrated with their existing infrastructure, delivered with high-touch project management from one client-focused team from the sale through delivery fulfillment.
  • Innovation Partner:  Client wants a customized approach for a complex project with high strategic business impact and risk from business-oriented thought leaders and industry experts with a deep relationship-based understanding of the client.

In a Value for the Money context, the opportunity is likely to be fairly straightforward and well defined by the client. In this case, this task may be optional since no additional analysis is required to identify the opportunity. In the case of Trusted Supplier or Innovation Partner, opportunities may initially present themselves in a more abstract form, with additional analysis necessary to clarify the opportunity. In some cases, the opportunity may be complex or multi-faceted and lead to multiple opportunities. This task is intended to conduct such analysis for simple to reasonably complex opportunities. For highly complex opportunities that are transformational in scope, this task will gather basic information for use in a subsequent transformation roadmap activity.

The CVM Opportunity Plan (Buying Behavior Planner section) should be consulted for the team’s analysis of the Buying Behavior for this opportunity, including the key value drivers and expectations that must be considered in the design and development of the solution.  This Buying Behavior Planner may also contain insight on the IBM team’s anticipated approach and response to each client expectation to be reflected in the proposal and the delivered solution.

There is not a single right approach to conducting such analysis. Consequently the Technical Solution Architect will have to evaluate the various tools and approaches available and select the most appropriate approach. CVM Opportunity planning is a logical first choice for helping to identify the opportunity. More information on possible tools and approaches is available in the associated guidance.

CVM Opportunity Planning

The CVM Opportunity Plan is designed to drive client value in each of the CVM phases and to provide updated insight about the opportunity to the IBM Team. In some cases, the Opportunity Plan may satisfy all of the requirements of this task. Opportunity planning begins in CVM Phase UNDERSTAND and is supported by a set of best practices, activities and work products that guide the progress of the opportunity through the CVM Phases. The IBM strategy and tactical response is based on the Buying Behavior, value drivers and client expectations for this opportunity. Consequently, the planning activities may take several forms - based on the Buying Behavior, size and scope of the opportunity.

The objectives for the key sections of opportunity planning are:

  1. Discuss and document critical information about the opportunity
  2. Identify the Opportunity Behavior for the opportunity and document the value drivers and expectations unique to the client situation; build the opportunity strategy based on these expectations that highlights our differentiation.
  3. Assess the strength of the opportunity by:
    - Identifying gaps in the sales and pre-delivery strategy
    - Determining the status of the solution design and proposal creation
    - Evaluating the decision to pursue, based on risk and impact and client expectations  
  4. Identify actions to execute the sales and pre-delivery strategies, and to implement the preferred solution in ways expected by the client. 
Steps
Identify Buying Behavior
Review CVM buying behavior profiles and select appropriate buying behavior for this opportunity. These profiles are published in IBM's Client Value Method. They are Value for the Money, Trusted Supplier and Innovation Partner.
Identify Primary Initial Client Imperative

Identify primary initial client imperative or need that states what the client is trying to achieve. This imperative or need is typically expressed by the primary sponsor for the engagement.

If this opportunity originated with the client's interest in one of the IBM Blueprint's Industry Imperatives, the Client Imperative represents a specialization of the Industry Imperative, tailored to suit a specific client's needs. In such cases, information associated with the Industry Imperative may be helpful in documenting the client imperative.

Select Opportunity Analysis Approach

Characterize the opportunity as simple vs. complex or highly complex (transformational in nature). Identify any tools described in the associated guidance that might be helpful in identifying a potential opportunity. For highly complex or transformational opportunities, execute the optional transformation roadmap activity and omit the final steps in this task.
Analyze Opportunity
Use any appropriate tools described in the associated guidance to analyze and clarify the opportunity. Although not required as an output of this task, consider using the Client Imperative artifact for more complex opportunities.
Define Opportunity

Define the opportunity by creating or elaborating in the Opportunity plan.

Key Considerations
Like other tasks in the Plan Phase of Team Solution Design, this is an on-going process.  If there is a teamroom or community used to store client information, the results of this task will be stored there for use on multiple projects.

Additional effort on behalf of a specific opportunity will typically be limited to what is required to design the solution and no more.
