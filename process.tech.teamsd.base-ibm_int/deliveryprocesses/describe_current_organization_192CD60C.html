Task: Describe Current Organization
This task will describe the parts of the client's organization that will be important to identify opportunities, potential sponsors, supporters and key resources.
Purpose

There are two relevant aspects of the client's organization to capture at this level: the formal organization as depicted in standard organization charts and the more subtle power model which shows who has power, who listens to whom, who is a figurehead. 

Later, during solution design, we will document in Task: Define Project the specific roles for that project (i.e., who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted or Informed).  At this point, the discussion of influence is within the organization at large, across many projects.

Main Description

Our process here will be similar as in Task: Understand Business Environment and Objectives.  That is, to proceed from the outside in.  Based on our understanding of the client's environment, their goals, issues, objectives and initiatives, we will limit our scope to the part of the client's organization that will be important to identify opportunities.  Our focus will be on potential sponsors, supporters and key resources. 

After requesting the standard organization charts, the interesting challenge is to sit with the client team and build the charts illustrating the ‘political’ influences and alliances as described above. 

The Determine Team IBM Relationship and Position section of the CVM Account Plan may have a view of the client contacts that may be helpful for this task. The format is shown in the Guidelines below.

See Current Organization Description for an example of how to annotate a PowerPoint organization chart with this power information. 

Key Considerations
Like other tasks in the Plan Phase of Team Solution Design, this is an on-going process. If there is a teamroom or community used to store client information, the results of this task will be stored there for use on multiple projects.

Additional effort on behalf of a specific opportunity will typically be limited to what is required to identify opportunities and no more.
