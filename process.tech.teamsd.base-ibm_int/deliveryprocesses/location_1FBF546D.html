WorkProductDescriptor
Work Product (Artifact): Location (ART 0574)
This artifact describes locations or structured collections of locations.
Purpose

The purpose of this artifact is to provide a description of the characteristics and capabilities of locations.

It may be developed in a wide range of different situations for different purposes. For example:

  • Within the context of Business and IT strategy-and-architecture projects, to identify the target set of locations the business and IT require to support the desired business capabilities.
  • Within the context of business operations, to optimise the property portfolio, and day to day space planning
  • Within the context of an IT system design and development project, to provide a back-drop for analyzing the current IT environment, for understanding the placement of users, for placing IT components and IT infrastructure, and for designing detailed IT infrastructure such as networks, systems management, site and facilities, and business continuity and recovery services.
  • Within the context of day-to-day IT operations, to support basic IT processes such as end-user support, problem management, change management, configuration management, inventory management, and asset management.

Description
Main Description

This artifact may be used to describe a single location, multiple locations, or structured collections of locations.

Brief Outline

The contents of this artifact will vary depending on the context in which it is being created. It might include:

  • A list of locations
  • For each location (or for each structured collection of locations):
    • Location name.
    • The geographic position of the location.
    • Details of business functions / business components / business users / business processes / business capabilities.
    • Details of IT functions / IT users / IT processes / IT environment and facilities / telecommunications environment and facilities / applications / data / IT infrastructure.
    • A description of geographic factors relevant to the project context; e.g. business environment, legislative environment, regulatory environment, culture.
    • A description of the location facilities; e.g. power, water, waste, heating, cooling
    • A classification of the location environment; e.g. office, data (or other IT) centre, warehouse, archive, retail, manufacturing, industrial processing
    • Proximity to and availability of resources; e.g. skilled workforce, communications, transport, raw materials, supplies, business partners, and customers.
    • Type of and details of ownership; e.g. freehold, lease
    • Financial details; e.g. running costs, maintenance costs, and capital costs, book value
      Occupancy.
    • A description of location security.
    • External business services that support the location.
    • Relative measures such as cost per square meter, space per desk, no. of users to desks, users per square meter, productivity, throughput, sales per square meter, stock per square meter, cost per square meter, power consumption.
    • Relationships with other locations
      • The supported IT system communication with other locations defined in terms of borders.
      • Business process interlocks and handovers.
      • IT process interlocks and handovers.
    • A list of structured collections or groupings of locations appropriate to the context
    • For each location collection
      • Location collection name
      • Location collection description
      • Location collection categorizations in terms of requirements and characteristics of the hardware and technology that the node collection represents.
Notation

This artifact is defined using text, structured tables, and matrices. 

Within the context of the operational aspect of the architecture of an IT system, the relationship between locations is expressed in terms of borders which show the communication support between locations.

The notation is defined by the IBM Architecture Description Standard Notation for Operational Model V2 and the IBM System Description Standard V3.
Key Considerations

This artifact may be developed to different levels of detail and contain different content depending on the context in which it is being developed or used.

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without this artifact:

  • It is not possible to understand the characteristics and capabilities of locations. It is difficult to optimize the placement of business and IT resources.
  • For an IT system, it is difficult to architect and design an IT system that is able to support users in multiple locations and deliver the required service level characteristics.
  • Location details are not available to adequately operate and manage the IT system and perform, for example, configuration, change, problem management.
Reasons for not needing
  • The business has only a few locations that all have very similar resources and characteristics.
  • All users of the IT system are co-located with the IT infrastructure.
Representation Options

This artifact can be represented in standard word processing tools (e.g. MS Word), or spreadsheets (e.g. MS Excel), or a database tool (e.g. MS Access).
