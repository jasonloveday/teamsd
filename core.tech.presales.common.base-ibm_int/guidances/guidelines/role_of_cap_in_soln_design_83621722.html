Main Description

With the inception of the IBM Business Blueprint, the term IBM Capability was introduced. In the simplest sense, a capability is defined in the Blueprint as “the ability to ….”. A generic example of a capability might be “The ability to measure risk exposures and solvency and capital requirements”. IBM Capabilities are intended to provide a concept of intermediate granularity between a product and an industry solution. IBM methods, including Enterprise Architecture also use the concept of Capability. Using the Capability concept to understand what the client is trying to achieve is a powerful conceptual tool that provides an additional perspective in solution design. This guidance focuses on our clients’ capability requirements but will also discuss how IBM’s capabilities relate to the clients’ capabilities.
