        Main Description

        In this task, various non-functional requirements and constraints are identified.  A non-functional requirement (NFR) is a quality requirement or constraint that a system (or some part of a system) must satisfy.  Qualities may be run-time (or observable) or non run-time (not observable). Service Level Requirements are considered runtime.  Constraints are usually imposed by the client and may be categorized as business or IT constraints. 

        Non-functional requirements are reflected as non-functional characteristics (NFCs) for various elements of the solution design.  Non-functional requirements are independent of our solution design while non-functional characteristics are related to design elements based on our specific solution design.  Each design element may have a pair of NFCs: the characteristic required to meet the NFR and the characteristic supported by the proposed solution based on the design choices made. 

        These required NFCs might also be described in terms of their importance (or our level of commitment to meet them with our design).  One possible way to do this is to use the MoSCoW classification scheme (see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MoSCoW  for more information).  That is: MUST (essential), SHOULD (important), COULD (nice to have and included), WON'T (but WOULD like in the future).  This approach makes sure that the requirements are very clearly articulated and agreed to, being clear about what is and what is not in scope.
        Steps
        Identify Non-Functional Requirements and Constraints

        Identifying the Non-Functional Requirements typically requires:

        • extracting requirements from existing documentation, including RFP, project vision statement etc.
        • interviewing end users and IT architects and other IT staff responsible for the project
        • interviewing client IT operations staff, especially with regard to constraints
        • investigating implied requirements of any imposed constraints
        • identifying non-functional requirements implied by use cases
        Analyze non-functional requirements

        Analyzing Non-Functional Requirements to define non-functional characteristics required:

        • assessing the implications of the non-functional requirements on non-functional characteristics for elements of the solution design
        • identifying the importance of non-functional requirements and characteristics
        • assessing the ability to support the required non-functional characteristics for elements of the solution design
        Validate Non-Functional Requirements and constraints with the client.
        Validate, with the client, your understanding of the requirements and constraints and discuss the implications on the solution design as they become known.
