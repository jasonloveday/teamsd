Task: Survey Candidate Assets and Optimize Solution Approach
This task is used in a pre-sale context to identify architectural assets that might be relevant for the project.
Disciplines: Asset Management
Purpose

The task, Survey Candidate Assets and Optimize Solution Approach, in a pre-sale context is used to:

  • Identify architectural assets that might be relevant for the project.
  • Analyze the fit and gap between assets and project requirements.
  • Decide whether to base areas of the solution on available assets.
  • Identify cost-complexity patterns based on prior lessons learned.
  • Optimize solution design approach to ensure alignment with client needs and earlier time-to-value.

      Main Description

      Now that you have an Initial Sketch of the Architecture Overview, use this list (based on the Asset Architecture Board's list of inhibitors to asset reuse) to survey Candidate Assets:

      • Understand the requirements of the environment for which assets are being considered.  That is, identify the need.
      • Understand the project scope and the general functionality required.
      • Understand the types of assets that may deliver value to the solution design process.
        • The most common types of assets usually considered are Architectural assets such as: Reference Architectures, Asset Architecture Board Case Studies, Solution Briefs, and previous client solutions.
        • There are many other technical sales accelerators that may prove valuable including: Demos, Challenge Brief, Solution Kits, Points of View presentations, Code assets, and Expertise (resources).
      • Use apps that curate solution and sales accelerator assets to optimize your search for potential assets (refer to the Guideline listed below: Source of Candidate Assets for Team Solution Design Users). Additional resources to consider are the Technical Collaboration Hubs and Industry Collaboration Hubs. Become aware of some possible assets to consider, find and acquire them.
      • If the curated assets do not fully satisfy the need, search through IBM asset repositories (see guideline below), industry literature, and other resources to identify assets or similar projects.  
      • Analyze the fit gap.  That is:
        • Assess whether candidate assets could contribute to solving the key challenges of the current project, and are compatible with its constraints. 
        • Analyze the extent of fit between each asset and the requirements (client and IBM). 
        • Consider whether any of the requirements are negotiable (to enable use of the asset). 
        • Find support and guidance to understand the asset, trust it, and apply it.
        • Assess whether the asset could be modified or extended to satisfy requirements.
      • Assess the trade-offs in cost, risk and functionality from adopting the asset.

      As a result, decide in principle whether to use one or more assets, and document the rationale for this decision in the Candidate Asset List.

      One way to accomplish this task is to hold a Cross Brand Solution Optimization workshop (known as Cross Brand SOP workshop).  Solution Optimization goes beyond the traditional quality review focus on viability and completeness to consider:

      • Maximizing the attractiveness of the solution; and
      • Minimizing the cost to IBM (and help to reduce price).

      A Cross Brand SOP workshop applies proven Optimization Patterns to help identify alternative approaches or re-use opportunities.  It brings our best people to bear earlier in the project to help shape better solutions.  The Cross Brand SOP workshop is, therefore, a formal checkpoint for reuse as well as a broader opportunity for optimization with these objectives:

      • to reduce complexity of the architecture. Primarily through examination of requirements for the minimum that is required and for simpler ways to address the requirements
      • to eliminate potentially redundant components
      • to identify assets that can be used to accelerate design or development
      • to simplify the design, development and deployment of the solution.

      A natural place for Cross Brand SOP is just after the requirements are reasonably well known and an initial high-level solution design has been determined.  So, Task: Survey Candidate Assets and Optimize Solution Approach is an appropriate point in the pre-sale solution design process. Results of the workshop will need to be reflected in Project Definition, Architectural Decisions and, probably, Architecture Overview.

      There are assets, guides and patterns to help the facilitator run the workshop. We encourage the use of outside experts to provide "another set of eyes" on the solution. These should be DE's or senior architects who have industry experience, experience with assets in the domain and good architectural skills.

      The other way to approach this to not run a formal workshop but to be familiar with the patterns and the technical guide. And to apply them as you're developing the architecture and design. A small working group meeting or review may be appropriate. This approach relies on you to adopt a Solution Optimization way of thinking.

      The primary source for information about Solution Optimization workshops is the Cross Brand SOP Wiki:

      https://w3.ibm.com/connections/communities/service/html/communityview?communityUuid=9f9464db-6b31-4f03-a1f0-dcb51599076b

      There you will find a wealth of information, including: educational options for SOP workshop leaders and participants, assets and sample results, and a list of available workshop leaders. 

      As you evolve your architecture and design, you should continually be evaluating the optimization of the solution, keeping focused on the key client value and avoiding unnecessary cost.
