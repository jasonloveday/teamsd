Task: Conduct Viability Assessment
This task will make an initial assessment of the viability of the solution to deliver the proposed Client Value. The viability assessment captures the known risks, assumptions, issues and dependencies.
Disciplines: Technical Architecture
Purpose

The purpose of an initial planned Task: Conduct Viability Assessment is:

  • To qualify the opportunity, that is, assess if the opportunity qualifies for further investment by IBM and the client.
  • To make an initial assessment of the viability of the solution ensuring that the solution lies within the "art of the possible".
  • To identify unrealistic or challenging requirements as early as possible, and seek to re-negotiate them.

        Main Description

        Viability assessments are done continually during the project but this is the first "official" task in Team Solution Design to conduct one.  We will continue refining and clarifying the "probability of success" of deploying the Solution as more decisions are made and further information becomes available.  This review process is done by the Technical Solution Architect, perhaps with other project team members and a peer from outside the team.

        In conducting a Viability Assessment, we consider what we learned about the client as we identified the opportunity and defined the project, including:

        • Business and IT initiatives and motives
        • Client success criteria and value drivers for the Buying Behavior for the project
        • Expectations of key client personnel including the sponsor accountable for the results and others responsible
        • The most important functional and non-functional requirements 
        • Ambitious development, implementation or deployment schedules (by phase)

        If serious inhibitors to success are discovered, reconsider the whole approach to the project. Re-iteration of previous tasks may be needed.  If very serious risks are discovered, contribute this insight to the CVM Opportunity Plan and consider focusing on another project.

        The Opportunity Leader will consider the input provided and consider a) any impact to Client Value and b) where there is Risk in IBM’s ability to deliver. Depending on the relative complexity and TCV of the opportunity, based on criteria set by the IBM brand leading the client proposal, the Opportunity Leader and Technical Solution Architect will seek peer review with experts and the appropriate Quality Assurance activity.

        For further information on Quality Assurance and roles such as Risk Managers, End-To-End Delivery Owners, and other Quality Practices, please see the TeamSD web site "Where To Get Help" page under the Quality Practices tab:http://w3-03.ibm.com/support/tsdesign/help7a.html

        Steps
        Qualify Opportunity
        Decide if there is an opportunity here for IBM and the customer that qualifies for further investment.
        Perform an initial viability assessment
        Identify appropriate participants.  List and prioritize project risks, assumptions, issues and dependencies. 
        Review the requirements
        Pay attention to the functional requirements and their relation to the Client Value that is required to make the investment viable for the Client. From an IT Delivery perspective also assess the non-functional requirements and determine the likelihood of implementing a system which meets the requirements including the timeframe and budget the client expects
        Consider whether requirements can be re-stated to make them more feasible
        For example:  Does continuous availability really apply to the whole system, or just a critical subset? Are there days of the week or month when 23 hours a day be adequate instead of 24?
        Resolve any viability issues
        Focus on any risks of project failure.
        Include information on current solution
        Identify information to measure improvement after implementation, if applicable.
        Perform a peer review
        A review by peers is very useful at this stage.  Involve a delivery participant from IBM and/or the client.  Involve the Risk Manager if assigned.
        Key Considerations
        In pre-sale solution design, the Viability Assessment provides a way to document the status of the project. Together with Project Definition and Architecture Overview, it is an important work product for communication with clients and IBM colleagues. Besides capturing project risks, the TeamSD version of Viability Assessment documents issues, assumptions and dependencies.
