Task: Refine Viability Assessment
This task will refine the Viability Assessment in order to ensure that the solution still lies within the "art of the possible".
Disciplines: Technical Architecture
Purpose

At this point, most of the decisions about the technical solution have been made.  Although viability assessments are continually done throughout the duration of the project, the purpose of an "official" Task: Refine Viability Assessment is to:

  • Ensure that the solution still lies within the "art of the possible".
  • Identify unrealistic or challenging requirements, and re-negotiate them.
  • Evaluate the business and organizational impact of the solution.  Assess if this will inhibit the success of the technical solution.
  • Review the solution, including Architectural Decisions that have been made, uncovering any risks that can't be mitigated.
  • Assess if a proposal of the solution should be formally reviewed in Task: Evaluate Integrated Solution. 

If very serious inhibitors to success are discovered, reconsider the whole approach to the project. Re-iteration of previous tasks may be needed.

      Main Description

      Viability assessments are done continually during the project but this is the second "official" task in Team Solution Design to conduct one.  This task is used to refine the Viability Assessment, clarifying the "probability of success" of deploying the IBM solution.  For example, the Viability Assessment is updated with solution review recommendations, Proof of Capability results, customer acceptance evaluation and for each major version of the operational model and the component model.
      In refining the Viability Assessment, we consider the known requirements and the decisions we've made in designing the solution, including:

      • A weighted evaluation of the available alternatives, weighted regarding functional and non-functional requirements, as captured in the Architectural Decisions 
      • The current high probability/impact risks for which we have no acceptable mitigation plan such as a design review, prototype, proof-of-capability, pilot, reduced scope or a phased approach
      • High priority issues which are unlikely to close before significantly impacting the project
      • Assumptions we have low confidence in and are of high impact -- raise these as risks
      • External dependencies which will have a negative effect on the plan

      Consider the scope and success criteria for this project and analyze risks and issues, such as:

      • Lack of access to knowledgeable users
      • Inexperience of project staff
      • Unclear or uncommitted sponsorship
      • Reliance on unproven business partners, assets, hardware or software products
      • Problems setting up the development and testing environment
      • Performance of the finished system
      • Ambitious development, implementation or deployment schedules (by phase) 
      Steps
      Review the current project work products
      Review the current project work products including the Project Description and Architectural Decisions to assess the viability of the system. Use peers and other experts to review work. 
      Update the Viability Assessment
      Update the Viability Assessment based on the most complete and recent information.
      Identify any significant risks
      Identify any significant risks and escalate them to teammates and appropriate management.
      Key Considerations

      The Viability Assessment provides a way to document the status of the project.  Together with Project Description, it is the primary vehicle for communication with clients and teammates.  Besides capturing project risks, the Team Solution Design version of Viability Assessment documents issues, assumptions and dependencies.
