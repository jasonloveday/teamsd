Task: Sketch Initial Architecture Overview
Based on the known client requirements, sketch an Architecture Overview that captures the initial direction for the Solution Design
Purpose

        Main Description

        The Architecture Overview can be sketched early in your Solution Design. This will enable both discussion with the client and with a proposal team on the direction you are proposing for the Solution Design and how it aligns to the win themes and IBM's ability to deliver.

        At this stage you are looking to begin to shape the Solution Design and relate it to some of the key requirements that you have captured, without representing it as a definitive view. Useful inputs for this initial sketch are:

        · The project goals – as captured in the Project Definition.

        · The functional and non-functional requirements as currently expressed – whether high level or in a detailed RFP

        · Your knowledge of the client’s systems, the integration that may be needed (as captured in the System Context)

        · The primary data stores that are relevant to the architecture – new or existing.

        Be conscious of your starting assumptions as you sketch the architecture overview. The type of questions you should ask include:

        · Is there a prior solution design or a reference architecture that you are leveraging?

        · Are you sure it is fully applicable in this case?

        · How much of the solution can you achieve with existing products and where do the requirements need some custom components?

        While the sketch may be high level it should represent a view of the scope of the solution and the integration boundary to existing systems. Consider the components measured above and the delivery channels for the solution as well as the information flow.

        Consult the TeamSD example Architecture Overview to determine the different Architecture Overview Views (Enterprise, Service and IT System) will be most effective. A sketch of both the Enterprise View and IT System View is optimal to communicate to both business and technical audiences.

        Test your sketch of the Architecture Overview to see if you can relate each area to a high level requirement and remove elements that can’t clearly be justified.

        Most of all, be bold and capture your initial ideas in this sketch so that you can gain feedback and insight from the client and team members on how the solution can evolve. You will certainly want to further elaborate this work product, but don’t wait for it to be perfect before you share.

        Now that you've got your sketch to the point where you think you've captured the client requirements, the design input from your team and the future needs, it's a good time to pause for a moment to consider if you're going in the right direction and you are focused on the most important elements of the solution.

        With this sketch of the Architecture Overview, you are ready to begin optimizing the cost and client value of your solution design.

        Key Considerations

        Sharing an early Sketch of the Architecture Overview will help you gain feedback on the initial direction for the Solution Design.  It also facilitates early discussion of potential Assets.

        Early model of costs: This is a perfect time to take an initial look at the cost of the solution.  Each major component should be reflected in a high level cost model, that should also include the major phases of design, development, test and deployment.  In many cases you will not have accurate numbers from your team but you can make assumptions and apply standard rules of effort estimation by phase to get an idea of the costs.  It is important to get this rough cost model built early (and not when you are about to submit a proposal) so the bid team can discuss the fit to client budget and/or expected winning price.

        Alternatives
        If the client has specified an Architecture Overview in their request to IBM for a propsal, you should begin with that and note changes you recommend.
