Guideline: Opportunity Identification Tools and Approaches
This guideline provides direction in a pre-sale context regarding the type tools available to assist in identifying an opportunity.

Main Description

This document contains a list tools and approaches that may be used to help analyze and identify complex and multi-faceted opportunities.

CVM Opportunity Planning

If CVM is being followed, it should result in an Opportunity Plan. In some cases, the Opportunity Plan may satisfy all of the requirements of this task. Opportunity planning begins in CVM Phase 1 and is supported by a set of best practices, activities and work products that guide the progress of the opportunity through the CVM Phases, while also being responsive to the Opportunity Behavior that fits the occasion. Consequently, the planning activities may take several forms - based on the Opportunity Behavior and size and scope of the opportunity:
Objectives: 

  1. Discuss and document critical information about the opportunity
  2. Identify the Opportunity Behavior for the opportunity and document the value drivers and expectations unique to the client situation; build the opportunity strategy that highlights our differentiation.
  3. Assess the strength of the opportunity by:
    - Identifying gaps in the sales and pre-delivery strategy
    - Determining the status of the solution design and proposal creation
    - Evaluating the decision to pursue, based on risk and impact and client expectations  
  4. Identify actions to close the gaps and execute the sales and pre-delivery strategies

Customer Challenge Brief

Although some opportunities may present themselves as highly technical, others may be expressed in more abstract terms at a higher level or even in business terms. The Customer Challenge Brief is a reusable asset based on a template that may be used to define pervasive customer challenges in a specific industry or IT domain in non-technical terms. The challenge is supported by pain points, strategic motivation and a solution strategy.  Examples of completed challenge briefs for selected customer challenges may be found in iRam. After using the iRam link, it will be necessary to log on to iRam to see the results of the query contained in the link

Note: The Customer Challenge Brief is a superset of information contained in the Client Imperative (ART 0696) UMF artifact. The Customer Challenge Brief is intended to document highly pervasive challenges for reuse across many clients. The Client Imperative UMF artifact is intended for use in a specific client situation when developing a transformation roadmap.

Develop Transformation Roadmap activity in TeamSD.

This optional activity in TeamSD can be used to decompose a large multi-faceted transformation opportunity into specific individual opportunities. 

Discovery - PoV - Client Workshop :
