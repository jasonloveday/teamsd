Main Description

An architectural strategy represents an essential course of action to necessary to satisfy what the client is trying to achieve (the Client Imperative). Development of architectural strategy requires broad consideration and selection of fundamental architectural alternatives that best meet clientâ€™s requirements. Architectural alternatives transcend products and technical specifications and describe fundamental approaches in computing. The IBM Transformation Framework provides a comprehensive context for evaluating where architectural alternatives should be considered. Ultimately, the selection among alternatives represents architectural decisions that must contribute to solution viability. This guidance provides insight into Cognitive Computing, a fundamental architectural alternative.
