Guideline: Solution Design Recipes
This guideline provides and overview of a set of guidance assets being presented to (and contributed by) the field to help accelerate supported scenarios.
Main Description

What’s going on today?

In today’s global economy, disruptions abound and change is the norm. As our clients look to transform their businesses or to optimize their IT environments or to drive innovation, we need to engage with them and help them with their strategies and challenges. In essence, become their trusted advisors.

However, with emerging trends and continuous change, we are in constant need to stay abreast of them as well as to understand the implications to our clients and their industries. How do we balance this information overload, make sense of it and not be overwhelmed; and yet still can provide an integrated IBM value proposition?

Hence the introduction of guidance in the form of “recipes”.

What are Solution Design Recipes?

Recipes are scenario-specific prescriptive guidance with outcomes, elements or “ingredients” and steps on what to do. This will help us to understand our clients’ needs and challenges, and to create innovative and relevant solutions to solve their problems.

These recipes will leverage the "IBM IC basket" – consisting of approaches, methods, best practices, frameworks, reference architectures, assets, tools, industry solutions and offerings. It will also include the use of Open source/Open standards where applicable.

We’ve created the recipes in mind maps for easy usage.

  • Each recipe will have defined outcomes.
  • Each recipe will include a list of elements.
  • Each recipe will have a list of steps, with hyperlinks to detailed information, accelerators, etc where available.

Recipes can be created for a variety of scenarios - anything that can help us do a better job of understanding and engaging with our clients. For example, they can be associated with pre-sales activities, customer-specific proof of concepts, IBM industry solutions and offerings, solution designs, innovative proposals etc.

To locate recipes that you can exploit or to contribute a recipe that you have created or refined, visit the official recipe subcommunity.
