Artifact: Service Model (ART 0582)
This artifact aggregates the Service Portfolio, Service Dependencies, and Service Specifications, mainly by captuing the control information regarding its various sub-artifacts.
Domains: Functional
Work Product Kinds: Model
Purpose

The purpose of this artifact is to provide a container for the service model and its sub-artifacts. Elements pertaining to the overall governance of the service model will be maintained here.

Note for pre-sale solution design (Team Solution Design users): In a pre-sale context, all elements of the Service Model sub-artifacts are usually not necessary to define a few existing or proposed services.  Although reference to the sub-artifacts and associated guidance may be helpful, a simplified template is provided that incorporates the subset of information from those sub-artifacts that is typically necessary in a pre-sale context.  See Service Model Template for Team Solution Design.

Description
Main Description

This artifact and its sub-artifacts capture details about Services including service hierarchy (functional classification), service exposure (exposure classification), service composition and dependencies, and service specification. As an aggregate work product, this artifact captures various pieces of informaton about services in the portfolio, including a name, a short description, a mapping to functional area, process, existing asset, (with the one that led to first identification in bold or with asterisk), exposure state and decisions (Candidate, Expose), etc.

The Service Model is a core work product in SOMA, updated and used by many activities. Service Model sub-artifacts are updated throughout the Identification, Specification, and Realization phases of the service lifecycle.

Note for pre-sale solution design (Team Solution Design users): Although the pre-sale solution architect is unlikely to create all of the sub-artifacts of Service Model, it is important to understand what they are and how they are used. Table 1 describes all sections of the Service Model artifact including its three sub-artifacts, namely the Service Portfolio artifact, Service Dependencies artifact and the Service Specification artifact. Only the four sections outlined in red, are typically required in a pre-sale, Team Solution Design context. Refer to the Service Model Template for Team Solution Design to see additional information on these four sections of the Service Model.

Brief Outline

This artifact maintains control information about its various sub-artifacts. It maintains the ownership and versioning information' related to the various sub-artifacts that together capture the service model.

Service Model Control Information

  • Define the scope, owner and version for the service model in this section.

Service Portfolio Control Information

  • Define the ownership and completion information of the Service Portfolio sub-artifact

Service Dependencies

  • For each set of service dependencies define the ownership. Each set of service dependencies will be captured in a sub-artifact.

Service Specification

  • For each set of service specifications define the ownership. Each set of service specificaitons will be captured in a sub-artifact.

Key Considerations

Note for pre-sale solution design (Team Solution Design users): For many projects that involve a Service Oriented Architecture, the identification and specification of services will not be required. For example, the SOA infrastructure or applications may already exist. Or the project might involve product or package integration. But when existing services must be analyzed or new services defined, this artifact will be very helpful.

For a discussion on the necessity of this work product and appropriate levels of elaboration for a pre-sale environment, consult the guideline: Pre-sale Work Product Usage for Team Solution Design. 

Tailoring
Impact of not having

Without this artifact and its sub-artifacts it would be difficult to specify the details of the services that have to be realized and implemented and to document the service realization decisions. This would lead to haphazard construction of Services and a set of Services that are potentially poorly matched with the business requirements.

Reasons for not needing

This artifact and its sub-artifacts are not needed if either the services are not externalized, or if the externalized services are very simple and the non-functional requirements for them are not stringent.
