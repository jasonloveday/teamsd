Main Description

The Subsystem Analysis work product is comprised of two major parts: a Subsystems overview and relationships section, and one or more Subsystem descriptions sections. During Subsystem Analysis, a Subsystem descriptions section is complete for each subsystem that is being analyzed and specified. The Subsystems overview and relationships section is completed incrementally, by revising and extending it as each subsystem is documented.

For each subsystem, plan for 4-8 hours for a work session to fill in one instance of the templates shown in 3.2 Subsystem descriptions. A typical work session will include a facilitator and a scribe, plus appropriate subject matter experts. In some cases one person may be able to act as both facilitator and scribe.

An additional 2-4 hours should be allowed for finalization of the content in the Subsystem descriptions section after the work session. Allow for an additional 1-3 hour to revise the Subsystems overview and relationships section, accounting for new relationships among subsystems that may have been identified.

The duration and the amount of effort required to develop the Subsystem Analysis work product will vary depending on:

  • Size of enterprise
  • Complexity of enterprise
  • Scope of engagement
  • Quality and completeness of the input work products
  • Experience of team (in technique and industry)
  • Contractual requirements for specific checkpoint or validation activities related to deliverable turn over.
