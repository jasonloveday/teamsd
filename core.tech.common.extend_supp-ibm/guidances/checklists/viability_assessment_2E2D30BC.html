Checklist: Viability Assessment
This checklist provides an extensive set of questions that can be used as a guide when validating technical solutions. For items that fail, develop a Risk Ranking Worksheet to understand the relative impact. Items that pose a significant probability of loss are addressed in the Findings and Recommendations section of the Viability Assessment.

Check Items
Requirements

Analysis and architecture transformation ensures that the quality of the analysis and architecture are passed on to the design.

  • Does a validated business process model exist so that we can identify opportunities for use cases and so that the use case model is not overburdened with business information?
  • Have requirements been identified and documented?
  • Are the requirements reasonable considering the "state of the art", budget, schedule, skills, or client infrastructure and capabilities?
  • Is it clear how the solution design  supports the requirement models?
  • Are all of the requirements supported by the solution design?
  • Are requirements specific, measurable and self-consistent?
  • Is it clear how the solution design supports the analysis models?
  • Is it clear how the solution design supports the design models?
Review Package Completeness
  • Are the non-functional requirements included in the package?
  • Are the analysis models included in the package?
  • Are change cases included in the package?
  • Are the operational characteristics of the package understood?  Are they benchmarked?
  • Does the package provide interfaces to integrate with other systems?
  • Are the architecture documents to be reviewed in the package?
Technical Stability, Complexity, Integrity, Security, and Architectural Qualities
  • Has the solution design considered the main aspects of the problem?
  • Does the solution support the project's goals, service levels and technical deliverables as defined in the statement of work?
  • Is the solution design consistent with the customer's IT standards or IT Architecture?
  • Has a risk assessment for information security been completed for the solution i.e., Critical Assets, Threats, Vulnerabilities, Risk (The likelihood that a given vulnerability will be exploited by a particular threat and the Impact), and Countermeasure.
  • Is the solution design consistent with the customer's information security risk model or posture?
  • Is the customer prepared to adopt all proposed and approved technologies?
  • Is there a customer commitment to sustain the vision of the system?
  • Are anticipated changes to the design addressed and supported in the solution design?
  • Do the architectural decisions have enough justification?
  • Has data integrity been considered from a solution design perspective? 
  • Has data persistence been considered from a solution design perspective? 
  • Has data concurrency been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Have transaction processing speeds and capacities been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Have transaction types such as nested and long-running transactions been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Has the scope of logic units of work been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Have batch-processing capabilities been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Has restart and recovery been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Have communications been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Has networking support been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Has security (confidentiality, Integrity, and availability) been considered from a solution design perspective?  If so, are all of the following areas addressed:
    • Trusted Credential: generation, distribution and management of identity and permissions (i.e., Identity Management, User Registration/Enrollment, Dis-enrollment, Provisioning, Security Objects (Tokens/Passwords/etc), LDAP/DB2 Data stores, Certificate, etc.).
    • Access Control: the gating of access to processes and services (i.e., Authentication, Authorization and Access Control).
    • Information Flow Control: the control and protection communications and transactions (i.e., Firewalls, Routers, 802.1x, Encryption In-transit/VPNs, Network Segmentation),
    • Integrity/Resilience: maintain the correct and reliable operation of critical components and processes (i.e., Intrusion Detection/Prevention, Input/Output Controls, Antivirus, Business Continuity and Recovery Plans, Encryption at rest).
    • Logging and Monitoring: capturing, analyzing, reporting, archiving and retrieving audit log records of events and conditions within a solution.
  • Has authentication been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Has scalability been considered from a solution design perspective?
  • Can the tests to assess architectural options against the requirement criteria be realistically performed?
  • Have tests with known values been defined for benchmarking?
  • Are all design decisions, which require global coordination across the project included in architecture decisions?
  • Are the classic trade-off questions pertinent to this system (for example, performance, flexibility, extensibility, availability, reliability, security and so on) been addressed?
  • Do all of the architectural decisions deal with global issues and not just simple functional requirements?  Decisions made during functional design tend not to be global and thus not to have architectural significance (they are "what" vs. "how" questions).
  • Does the architectural decision or policy have broad and long-lasting applicability and must apply to the system under development? 
  • Is the architecture being effectively communicated to those who need to know?
  • Does the solution design foster inter-operability and data exchange?
  • Have the appropriate metrics been defined?
  • Are there plans or have high-risk solution components been identified and prototyped?
  • Are the selected solution components viable and generally available?
  • If a system management plan is necessary, is it sufficient and complete?
  • Are the selected solution components proven in an environment with similar requirements and constrains?
Architectural Traceability
  • Check the requirements work products to ensure that enough statements were extracted to form an adequate basis for traceability.
  • Verify that acceptance test cases have been stated where applicable.
  • Verify that all tests in the Acceptance Test Plan, Usability Test Cases and System Test Plan are traceable to the requirements.
  • Have the traceability results been validated with the customer to show that the system is providing the required capabilities?
  • Has duplicate trace information been avoided?
  • Are all of the related decisions tied to their source (problem statement, non-functional requirements, and so on) so that if the source elements change, the decisions derived from them can be reconsidered easily?  (This provides traceability from requirements into the implemented system).
Operational Model
  • Is the operational model specification complete?  Does it include: hardware platforms, system requirements, operating systems, connections, network protocols, network requirements, and language runtime?
  • Is the operational model consistent with each of the non-functional requirements and architectural decisions?
  • Was an approach used to verify the model's ability to deliver the appropriate service level characteristics?
  • Is the operational model consistent with the Deployment Units model?
  • Are all the prerequisites for hardware, software and networking performance and specifications documented?
  • Have walkthroughs been performed to ensure that operational and system management scenarios are covered (e.g., restoring a key database, adding a new server, etc.)?
  • Has the operational model considered system management, backup and recovery and security needs?
Component Model
  • Have walk-throughs of the component model been performed to make sure that it supports each of the Use Cases?
  • Are all component model APIs defined?
  • Are processes for managing changes to API definitions in place?
  • Has the component partitioning been checked for optimal decoupling of components?
  • Has the frequency and size of inter-component communications been minimized?
  • Do all components have well-defined interfaces?
  • Are proven architectural patterns considered and appropriately used?
  • Are the dependencies between components minimized?
  • Is the subsystem application independent as much as possible?
  • Are major component interfaces at all levels (including user interface, work station connection, local area network, distributed servers, wide area network, host and database) defined and maintained?
  • Consistency – Are all parts of the architecture in harmony?  Are there common approaches to partitioning and allocation?
  • Well-formedness – Is the architecture well defined and all aspects can be logically related?  Are all descriptions precise, accurate and traceable to formal constructs?
  • Orthogonality – Are the architectural components independent?
  • Generality – Do the system components work in other contexts as much as possible?
  • Extensibility – Can the architecture be extended?
  • Transparency - Can the system be used without knowing its implementation?
  • Openness – Can components be modified and integrated in alternate ways?
Deployment Units
  • Are the non-functional requirements consistent with the operational model?
  • Are all components mapped onto deployment units?
  • Is the mapping between component models and deployment units clear?
  • Are all component interfaces defined in time to be used by the developers of other components?
  • Is the frequency and size of inter- component communications minimized?
  • Is the boundary of the system to be built explicitly represented in the deployment unit model?
  • Is each deployment unit understandable in isolation?
  • Are the deployment unit interfaces clearly and fully described?
  • Are major deployment unit interfaces at all levels (including user interface, work station connection, local area network, distributed servers, wide area network, host and database) defined and maintained?
Application Programming Interface
  • Do the API's conform to Coding Guidelines?
  • Check the documentation of APIs intended for publication for conformance with appropriate publication guidelines.
  • Depending on the intended audience for the API and its complexity, verify that documentation includes scenarios and sample code showing how the API may be used.
  • Check API for appropriate levels of completeness and extensibility.
  • Check API for unnecessary exposure of internal data representations.
Ability to Deliver and Development Approach
  • Does the project team understand the approach for the current phase?  Are methods applied in an appropriate, consistent and comprehensive manner?
  • Are assumptions regarding the estimates, scope, or work products reasonable and valid?
  • Does the project approach contain activities to review the "table of contents" of all deliverables with the customer before the deliverables are built?
  • Will the project manager be able to determine when work is out of scope?
  • Are inspections, reviews or walkthroughs being conducted for requirements, analysis and design, architecture, and construction work products?
  • Is a usable and sufficient development environment in place (facilities, tools, and infrastructure)?
  • Are appropriate development processes in place and followed (version control, defect management, change management, release management and testing)
  • Are the project plans and estimates reasonable in light of the solution design?
  • Are there issues with defects in the solution with regards to seriousness or magnitude?  Can they be corrected in time to meet budget or schedule constraints?
  • Are the work products of acceptable quality and completeness?
  • Are the support and architecture functions funded and staffed?
  • Are the appropriate levels and numbers of technically skilled individuals available to the team?
  • Have organizational change readiness issues been addressed?
  • Have IT organizational skills gaps been suitably addressed?
  • Have IT change readiness issues been suitably addressed?
  • Is there a clear and viable migration path defined to get to the solution?
  • Given disruption to the customer's normal operation in-migrating to the solution, is there a contingency plan if initial migration to the solution does not work?
  • Are the appropriate system management disciplines in place for the solution?
  • Does the client have the capability to support the solution once IBM has completed the project?
  • Will the solution design initial and on going costs meet client budget and ROI objectives?  Does the project team understand the cost ramifications of the solution?
