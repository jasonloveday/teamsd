Artifact: Strategic Roadmap (BUS 326)
The Strategic Roadmap work product is a high level plan and timeline of initiatives (and/or key initiative activities) which were prioritized and selected as part of the e-business Strategy strategic portfolio.
Domains: Business Strategy
Work Product Kinds: Strategy
Purpose
Provides the ordering, phasing, sequencing and timing of initiatives and key activities which will set the scope and direction for changes to the Strategic Architecture (processes, organization, and IT) which are needed to realize the strategy.

Note to Team Solution Design users: In the context of a pre-sale engagement, the purpose of this artifact is to quickly develop a high level Transformation Roadmap of near term and strategic initiatives in response to defined client imperatives that reflect broad transformational change.

Description
Main DescriptionThe Strategic Roadmap work product is a high level plan and timeline of initiatives (and/or key initiative activities) which were prioritized and selected as part of the e-business Strategy strategic portfolio.

Note to Team Solution Design users: Within the context of Team Solution Design, the Strategic Roadmap will be referred to as the Transformation Roadmap. In developing a Transformation Roadmap, the purpose is develop a "initiative level" roadmap. The "portfolio level" roadmap is only required in this context if there are multiple transformation initiatives. In a pre-sale engagement, the Transformation Roadmap typically includes a single transformation initiative and is developed in a short time frame. A transformation Initiative describes a set of programs and associated changes that define how one or more tactics, as defined in The Client Imperative (ART 0696), will be realized by the client. Tactics in turn describe how the client imperative solution strategy will be realized. Note that terms such as imperative, strategy, tactic and initiative may need to be adapted to the client’s accepted terminology. Refer to Business Direction (BUS 311) for a definition of the terms strategy and tactic. A transformation initiative is comparable to the initiative described in Transition Initiative (BUS 326). Minimum information for the Transformation Roadmap would typically include the following.

  • Transformation initiative Overview which could include a final statement of the client imperative (including solution strategy).
  • Solution overview that reveals context and scope and how the solution strategy expressed in the client imperative would be realized by selected to-be changes included in the transformation initiative.
  • Description of the expected business benefits
  • Description of individual programs within the transformation initiative
  • Roadmap of recommended programs within the transformation initiative that illustrates suggested sequence and dependencies. Although  less likely in a brief pre-sale engagement, the roadmap could include elements of timing and resources.
Brief Outline

A Portfolio Level Strategic Roadmap generally includes the following content and information:

  • Prioritized strategic initiatives
  • Phasing and other sequential considerations
  • High-level timeline (initiatives grouped into milestones, such as quarters, semi-annual time blocks, annual horizons, etc.)
  • Summary of high level investment and benefits to coincide with timeline view

An Initiative Level Roadmap generally includes the following content and information:

  • Initiative to audience/value proposition/business capability-requirements
  • Relationship of initiative to process/business function
  • Content requirements to support initiative
  • Functional requirements to support initiative
  • Capability descriptions (process, content, organization, IT) – what it needs to do
  • Change requirements (process, content, organization, IT) – what changes need to occur
  • Key activities needed to make changes
  • Key Dependencies and interdependencies
  • Estimated time to complete activities, and overall timing

Key Considerations
  • Generally, a Portfolio Level Strategic Roadmap will be done at a high level.  This means that its intent is to provide a horizon perspective of time and how initiatives will be deployed across that horizon.
  • Conversely, an Initiative Roadmap is intended to be actionable.  This requires that sufficient detail be provided so at to facilitate analysis and development work.

Enterprise Architecture Influence: Existing enterprise architecture is an important influence on solution design. See the associated Guideline: The Role of Enterprise Architecture in Solution Design for guidance on EA artifacts (or their equivalents) that should be considered when developing this work product.
