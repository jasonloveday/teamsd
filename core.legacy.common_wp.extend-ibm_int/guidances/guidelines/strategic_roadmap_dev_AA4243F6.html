Guideline: Developing Strategic Roadmap

Main Description

Portfolio Level Strategic Roadmap:

  • Conduct workshops and work sessions with participants from the core team, and other individuals (extended team members) who are knowledgeable about the areas being pursued.
  • Review the Transition Initiatives to identify dependencies (critical path, interdependencies) and other factors to consider for phasing or timeline development.
  • Create a high level timeline including appropriate phasing of initiatives based upon previously identified dependencies and factors.
  • Revise the timeline based upon considerations and implications from the business justification.

Initiative Level Roadmap:

  • Conduct workshops and work sessions with participants from the core team, and other individuals (extended team members) who are knowledgeable about the areas being pursued.
  • Using previously completed Transition Initiatives work product content, identify the process/application impacted, and new/incremental content and functional requirements.
  • Identify high-level process, organization and IT capability descriptions that serve as design points for new business and IT architecture and infrastructure.
  • Define at a high level, the process, content, organization and IT change requirements needed to position the company to support and enable the initiative.
  • For each change requirement, identify the key activities needed to effect the change, along with an estimate of time needed (duration), and associated dependencies.
  • Using the key activities and implications identified previously, develop a work plan depicting the individual activities and timing (based upon the estimated duration, interdependencies, etc.).  In addition, establish and document the overall duration for the initiative (note: multiple projects may be suitable based upon the size and complexity of the initiative, and the areas affected).
