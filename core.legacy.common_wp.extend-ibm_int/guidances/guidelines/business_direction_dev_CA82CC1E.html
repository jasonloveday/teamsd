Main Description

Development of the Business Direction work product is usually based on an initial version of the clients’ vision, mission statement and business goals and CSFs.

Interviews with employees (operational and management) from the clients’ organization and business focus workshop with the clients’ executive team are deployed to assess validity, quality and level of shared views of the business direction. The workshop is also used to address and correct discrepancies in the business direction and to ensure a common understanding and buy-in of all members of the project team (both client and IBM).

To achieve the above the following critical items are assessed and reviewed, e.g.:

  • The coherence and perception of Mission statement, Vision statement, Business Goals and CSF's, e.g.:
    • How management is communicating this in the organization and externally (with attention to any accessibility needs in the delivery of the message).
    • Ensure alignment and consistency (i.e. the Goals support the Vision, the CSF's support the Goals, etc.).
    • Whether mission and goals are ‘lived’ in the organization.
  • Measurability and feasibility of the business goals and CSFs.  Relative importance and driving factors of business goals (CSFs), their impact on business success and executive ownership.

Where Business Direction is also described in terms of key value propositions and critical capabilities, the business focus workshop will also focus on the network of value attributes and capabilities (value creation web or strategic capability network) to identify the key value propositions.  And as such assessing and reviewing critical items, for example:

  • Quality and importance of the key value propositions and associated value attributes and critical business capabilities.
  • Ensure alignment and consistency with the various business direction elements.
  • Assess critical capabilities on high level process, organization and ICT impact and inconsistencies.

The outcome of the interviews and business focus workshop will usually be a set of sheets that are communicated to the project team (including the Steering Committee).

Developing Business Direction in ‘green field’ situations

When Business Direction is developed for a ‘green field’ situation - for example, addressing a new (e-) business opportunity for the client - the described approach in general terms still applies.  Although the starting point may differ and the development process may be more iterative, strong focus on the coherence among the various business direction elements as well as unambiguous specifications remains imperative for the success of the (new) business model.
