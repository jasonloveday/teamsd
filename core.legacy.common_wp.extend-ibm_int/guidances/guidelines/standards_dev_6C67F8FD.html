Main Description

In a workshop, the approach taken:

  • Agree a set of key technical principles relevant to the client's organization and potential future systems, and the options within each. The examples above describe some obvious ones, but there will be others, applicable to the client's environment, relating to specific technology or types of systems. Suggest no more than 15-25 are chosen.
  • Each principle should be mapped to each standard, and agreement reached on the implications. For some principles there may be no technical implications, for others implications in several areas. The conclusions are recorded in a matrix.
  • When all principles have been considered, the overall implications for each standard are evaluated. This should be done through debate and discussion, rather than numeric scoring, as some principles will carry greater weight than others.
  • If Standards are being developed in the context of an Enterprise Architecture engagement, the implications of each conclusion for the Enterprise Architecture are considered. This should be recorded as a table, similar to the example above, for input into further enterprise technology framework definition activities.

Specifically for developing security standards, perform:

  • A review of the organization’s business strategy and related security requirements.
  • A review of the organization’s IT strategy, current security concerns, and future security requirements.
  • A review and analysis of the organization’s current security policy and standards against the needs of the organization’s business and IT strategies.

Specifically for developing accessibility standards, perform:

  • A review of the “external to the company” government (ANSI (American National Standards Institute), ISO (International Standards Organization), Federal, State, Higher Education, etc.) and industry (W3C (World Wide Web Consortium), Intelligent Manufacturing Systems (IMS) Global Consortium, etc.) accessibility standards and regulations for the purpose of identifying those applicable to the organization’s business and IT strategies.
  • A review of the organization’s IT strategy, current accessibility concerns, and future accessibility requirements.
  • A review and analysis of the organization’s current (if any) accessibility policy and standards against the needs of the organization’s business and IT strategies.
