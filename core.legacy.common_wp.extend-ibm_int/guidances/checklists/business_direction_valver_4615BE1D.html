Checklist: Business Direction Validation and Verification
Relationships
Related Elements
  • Business Direction
Check Items
Vision statement describes desired state of the business that can be easily understood
  • Desired state of the business at a point in the future, usually 3-5 years out
  • Describe in present tense as if already achieved
  • Stated in a manner that can be understood by all employees (i.e. short, precise)
Values are clearly articulated and identify underlying ideals
  • Underlying beliefs or ideals that influence the way individuals think and behave
  • Clearly articulated, easy-to-understand
Mission statement is concise and descriptive
  • Concise statement on why the business exists
  • Describes what the management team is collectively paid to achieve
Goals are comprised of measurable elements with specific end points

Are high-level objectives which are owned by senior management

Critical success factors address single needs (one CSF per need)
Are key “few things that must go right.”
Strategies are measurable and address CSF's
  • Statements of principal approaches and actions to address the CSF’s
  • Measurable so as to allow progress to be tracked.
Management principles are clearly defined, but non-constraining
  • Guidelines that influence desired performance and work-related behaviors (comprised of key management philosophies and core values).
  • Clearly defined but non-constraining to provide appropriate latitude for decisions and actions.
